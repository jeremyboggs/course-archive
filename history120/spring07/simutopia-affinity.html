Simutopia: Affinity

We hold these truths…
We hold that a moral revival in these United States is possible without Puritanism; that is, without busybodies meddling into our personal affairs, without thought police controlling our intellectual life. We can attain a recommitment to moral values – without puritanical excesses…
We hold that law and order can be restored without turning this country of the free into a police state, as long as we grant public authorities some carefully crafted and circumscribed new powers…
We hold that the family – without which no society has ever survived, let alone flourished – can be saved, without forcing women to stay at home or otherwise violating their rights…
We hold that schools can provide essential moral education – without indoctrinating young people…
We hold that people can live in communities without turning to vigilantes or becoming hostile to one another…
We hold that the pursuit of self-interest can be balanced by a commitment to the community, without requiring us to lead a life of austerity, altruism, or self-sacrifice….
We hold that political officials can be civil and productive, while having different opinions, and still pursue the best interest of the entire community…
We hold that powerful special-interest groups in the nation’s capital, and in so many statehouses and city halls, can be curbed without limiting the constitutional right of the people to lobby and petition those who govern….
We hold these truths as affinitians, as people committed to creating a new moral, social, and public order based on restored communities, without Puritanism or oppression.
The Jacksonian Era was a very influential time in the history of the United States. Expanded suffrage, manifest destiny, and patronage are just a few of the key changes in the nation. However, the political landscape continued to follow the same path of partisanship. Party politics and sectionalism were the predominant problems of the era. My utopian society, Affinity, is structured to correct the problems associated with the individualistic nature of the previous era.
Since the formation of the Unites States, the resistance between political groups can be identified in each era. Jacksonian democracy was resisted by the Whig party. A major problem during Andrew Jackson’s presidency was the Nullification Crisis. It arose because of sectional differences concerning the Tariff of 1828. “Every state had numerous political factions, but they did not cross state lines.” The United States has consistently faced problems, because different factions try to pursue their own agenda, in the name of nation at large. If every citizen had more of a community-oriented mindset, it would eliminate many of the conflicts associated with individualism.
The edict written above is the cornerstone of my utopian society, Affinity. A nation grounded in these principles has an opportunity to achieve a better sense of unity and community values. Given that the constitution of the Unites States was written in 1787, dilemmas arise because it is relevant for modern times. In Affinity, the edict is shaped for the present era, but is updated by new generations to represent the entire community’s values. Since the members of Affinity share the same core beliefs, this task will not be difficult. Despite being community oriented, individualism is highly encouraged. Having community values while promoting individualism seems like a paradox, however if citizens have their own ambitions and desires while adhering to what is best for the community, it is possible. Having an entire community believe the same core values may seem impractical, but it is how most nations are. The United States does not demand that all the citizens believe in the same basic values, but still most citizens share the same common beliefs: the Bill of Rights lays out most of the beliefs. Affinity will endeavor to do the same.
Geographically, Affinity is designed around a square with Public Buildings in the middle, and Housing and Farming on the outside. Each member of the community has separate plots of land, equally spaced apart. There is farming land, for people to grow crops, and there are factories for other people to work at. The community is primarily capitalist based, but individuals are allowed, based on the edict of the community, to follow more traditional subsistence based living methods. Every citizen has the possibility of achieving their wishes, as long as they believe in the edict of the community. Politicians are very similar to modern times and the Jacksonian Era, although there are no political parties. They can have different ideas on how to serve the community, but instead of pursuing their own agenda, they work together to better serve the community. Affinity is a community shaped very similar to the United States today. The difference is there are clearly defined values and morals that the citizens believe and accept, as condition to live there. By doing what is best for the community, everybody is able to live in a realistic utopia.
