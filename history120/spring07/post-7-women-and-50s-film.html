Post #7: Women and 50’s Film

Throughout history, women have been characterized in many different ways, and unfortunately it is usually in a negative way. During the 1950’s, it was a popular stereotype that women were homemakers. Although this is not necessarily negative, many men expected this of their wives and women who did not fall into this stereotype were considered to be socially abnormal. Today a few different stereotypes permeate throughout our country, including women as purely sexual objects to women choosing to work neglating their families. Overtime, stereotypes may have changed but still do exist amongst women of today. The stereotypes of women have changed overtime from housewife to sexual objects, the main theme of women being subservient to men has continued throughout the generations.

While watching the video, “Cooking Terms!” there are many examples of the main stereotype women faced in that day and age as a homemaker. In the opening of the video, we are introduced to Tim and Margie, who are newlywed’s. Almost immediately we are introduced to the typical roles of husband and wife in the 1950’s; men went to work and women stayed home to cook. The video states, “The honeymoon is over for Tim now that he starts back to work. And it’s over for Margie as she enters her bright, new kitchen to cook her first meal. Yes, can she cook Tim’s favorite meals, just as his mother used to make?” The woman is expected to stay home and cook for her husband. Not only is she expected to cook for him, but also to make what he requests and up to his standards. In this scene, you can see how women were expected to please their husbands, with no real thought of themselves.

Another scene that promotes this idea of women being subservient to men occurs when Margie attempts to cook a cake for Tim. However, she misunderstands the cooking term to “cream” butter and the cake comes out wrong. The video states how Tim will be suprised and disappointed that Margie ruined the cake. Margie then appears in the scene to be very upset and worried that she cannot cook to please her husband. This is another example of the woman not doing something because she really wants to, but to please her husband. Because she incorrectly cooked the cake, her husband will be disappointed in her. You get the sense from this scene that because Margie cannot cook, she will not make a fit wife.

Finally, near the end of the video, Margie learns what all the terms mean and realizes there is a glossary in the back of her cookbook in case she has any more questions. The video states, “Now Tim will never have to know about how Margie ruined her first meal.” This statement again inforces the idea that a woman who cannot cook is regaurded as a failure in the eyes of her husband. The video even goes so far as to say this is one of the most important things you can do in your marriage and throughout your “career as a cook”. Therefore, obviously, you are not successful in your life as a homemaker if you cannot cook. These women were expected to please their husbands with their cooking skills and seemingly were considered failures if they could not do this task.

Although women today are not always expected to stay at home and cook for their husbands, there are still ways in which women are subjected to the idea that men are superior in status. For example, many music videos, especially from the rap industry, portray women to be purely glorified sexual objects, at the disposal of men. For example, in the Akon video “Smack That“, women are shown in skimpy clothing, dancing around for the men. Even the lyrics of the song are easily recognized as purely sexual, “Maybe go to my place and just kick it like Taebo/And possibly bend you over look back and watch me/Smack that all on the floor/Smack that give me some more/Smack that ’till you get sore/Smack that oh-oooh.”

Another example of women becoming subservient to men is in the workforce. Constantly there are stories arising of women doing the same work as men, however, they are payed less. Also, there are consistently lawsuits filed by women about being sexually harrassed by men in the workplace. One example of this is a video on YouTube entitled “Sexual Discrimination Educational Video“. This video does a great job of showing things that typically happen to women in the workplace, except it tries to make a joke of the entire thing. The opening credits read, “How to deal with whiny women”.

Another scene shows a man (presumably the woman’s boss), greeting her by name and then smacking her on the butt. The narrator then comes in to tell what he did “wrong”, and explains you should not call a woman by her name, but instead refer to women in pronouns or verbs such as “you” or “move”. While this video may be intended for humor, it is these attitudes that continue to degrade women in today’s society.

As you can see, while stereotypes of women have seemed to evolve over time, I believe the main focus has continued over time. Women are still expected in many cases to answer to men, whether it is in the household or, today, in the workplace. It is unfair and unjust that this discrimination continues into the 21st century. A woman should have the option to do what she wishes, whether it is to become a stay at home mom, or a CEO of a corporation.
