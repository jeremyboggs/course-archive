The Utopia of all Utopias

From 1828-1850, the Jacksonian Era defined the lifestyle in the United States, where unprecedented ventures and reform paved the way for the nation to transition from its current rural mentality into a more capitalistic, industrial, and urbanized way of life.

A utopia would consist of both a religious and socialist community. Religious in the sense that since people were becoming more religious, and not drinking and restricting sexual behavior, and Socialist in the sense that people were moving more towards industrialism and making money and economically succeeding. My utopia will be a commercial enterprise, because our main goal is to make alot of money to improve the standards of living and be better than everyone else. Yet, everyone does their share of equal work. The community supports itself by the majority of it being industrial, but there are still some agricultural, because the entire society has not transformed into industrial yet. Some are still holding on to their traditional agricultural roots and since we want the world to be perfect for each one of our citizens, we understand that about them. Our utopia will also be open to the rest of the world, because our main goal is business and richness so the more people there are that come into our world, the more money we will be makng. It will be urban in character yet there will be no slavery. It will be a little bit of both worlds because we are in transition period when people are going from rural to urban, so our main goal is to satisfy both sides.

Our utopia is also a profit seeking capitalist community that also holds religion in high regards. Our religious faith was has not been forgotten even though money has become our number one priority. For our religious rules; there are restrictions on sexual behavior and drinking, and this will be implemented by everyone. It is a close-knit community and everyone keeps an eye on everyone. All people work equally and have equal and set standards. In essence, everyone will work for their fair share and accumulate all their money in one place and distrubute it evenly amongst everyone.

A Board of Directors is technically the head of the town. They don’t rule over anyone but they meet once a week to hear and set any problems straight. They are who the town people go to incase of emergencies, they know everything that is happening. All members of the community meet once a week in any public building and work together or talk. The way it is set out on the map is, the farms and the people that still farm are set on one side of the river. They don’t think the same way the people who have moved to an industrial way of living think and live their life the way they want to but still would rather be in our utopia. They have their farms and their homes. On the other side of the river is the new “city life” way of living. The factories are set all over the place and homes are all around so that people could live close to where they work. There are parks for families to enjoy their time and publc buildings for the citizens of our utopia to have their needs met just incase they are not.
