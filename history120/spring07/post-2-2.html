Post #2

The use of images can be helpful in understanding history because it gives you an actual look at what happened during the event that is being depicted, however you must be careful with images because they may be a biased look at the event. The picture by Paul Revere showing the Boston Massacre shows the side of the American people involved in the incident. Before describing what the picture shows, we must understand what actually happened in the Boston Massacre. A barbers apprentice was yelling at a British soldier because he thought that the soldier had not paid his bill. The soldier had in fact paid it but paid no attention to the boy. The boy continued to yell at the soldier so the soldier clubbed him. This act wound up creating a large mob of angry Americans who through things at the soldiers. The soldiers then called in for reinforcements. A chunk of ice hit one of the soldiers and knocked him on the ground and he fired into the air as he fell. Apparently, someone had yelled “fire.” This led to the rest of the troops firing into the crowd (wikipedia). In the picture, Captian Preston is behind a line of troops with his sword raised ordering them to fire into the crowd of Americans. It seems to be depicting a hateful and uncalled for act by the Captian. In reality, one of the soldiers that was trying to keep the mob at bay. The troops are shown right next to the mob of Americans viciously gunning them down. There are huge clouds of smoke in the picture due to all the gunfire. The American crowd is larger than that of the British soldiers and is shown as completely innocent and scared. They seem to be cowering, trying to help the injured, and three dead mean on the ground.

This picture is being used to show the Americans being completely massacred, hence the name of the event, for no apparent reason. Revere must have wanted this picture to horrify Americans because he made some changes in the picture. First of all, he changed the name of the building behind the soldiers from Customs House to Butcher’s Hall. This makes the British seem like they are killing the Americans for no good reason. Secondly, the Americans are no retaliating what so ever and look completely innocent. Now nothing that I read says the Americans retaliated when the British started shooting, but they definitely were not being peaceful prior to the shots being fired. It seems Revere just wanted Americans to see this event as an innocent group of Americans were just walking around town and then some soldiers came and shot at them for no good reason. The British may not have been just in their actions, but it was not them just shooting Americans for no reason.
