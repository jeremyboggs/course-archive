Reading the Runaways

Running to freedom

It has been 200 years since we signed the Abolition of the Slave Trade Act on March 25, 1807. Yet we still have little idea on how much the slaves suffered and what their personal lives consisted of. Some tend to forget that they were families that had grandparents and children that were either slaves or never to be seen again. Some slaves had masters that took the chance and educated their slaves just a little hoping that it would allow them to work better, and some wanted to keep their slaves illiterate so they would never survive if they ran away. There were always runaway slaves and they came in all different types, from every skin color to literacy level, yet they all had one thing in common, the pursuit for freedom.

In the ads that were provided for us from the database The Geography of Slavery, I found that there were many different kinds of slaves that ran away. Some of them in groups and some alone. It was interesting for two reasons, one was because they were stating the qualities that the slave possessed and the other was that the slave had these qualities to begin with. They could read, write, play instruments, use tools that took skill and they were even well mannered and could speak proper English. That was very risky for the owner because they took the chance of loosing their slave. Someone could read the ad and could keep the slave knowing what they could do for them.

The ads helped me understand the history of colonial Americans slavery, it showed a side of slaves that wasn’t shown in most textbooks. The ads portrayed the slaves for who they were, not just a expected depiction of what they assumed slaves to be. From wikipedia it states that “Slaves are people who are held against their will since within capture, their purchase, or there birth, and are deprived of their individual freedom for the purpose of exploitation for their labor…”. That citation shows that slaves didn’t have a choice to become a slave, but they did have the choice to run away to a chance for freedom.

There were runaway slaves and they came in all different types, from skin color to literacy level, yet they all had one thing in common, the pursuit for freedom. In conclusion slaves had qualities that made them who they are. All different and unique to each person, but the common factor that they didn’t possess is freedom, and that is the same thing that puts them in the same category of runaway slaves.
