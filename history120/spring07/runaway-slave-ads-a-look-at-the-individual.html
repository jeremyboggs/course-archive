#1 Runaway Slave Ads: A Look at the Individual

The lives of slaves are often shrouded in mystery. Growing up as students, we are often taught the bear minimum of our country’s past. We learn the most important details, often excluding supporting facts and events, often excluding details of equal importance. Runaway ads help historians put these details together in a way to better understand the lives of slaves and the relationships they had with their owners.

Looking through runaway slave ads, one begins to get an idea of the extremely personal relationship between a slave and his owner. By reading these ads we learn about individuals and the skills they possess, including the most miniscule details. We learn that some slaves had real trades of which they possessed extensive knowledge. The more detailed ads include behavior traits, showing that slaves may not have had hardly any privacy. Living a life of constant supervision would definitely take its toll on a person, making escape a viable option. Slaves were forced to live a life of oppression where their only option of agency was to run away.

These ads enhance my understanding of colonial American history by giving slaves individual lives. We often learn about slaves as a group, working as a group, living as a group. We often think of slaves as less intelligent, although to no fault of their own with the obvious lack of education and social opportunity. These ads show that in a lot of cases this was not always the truth. Slaves had advanced knowledge of some skills; we learn from ads that some were excellent tailors or excellent blacksmiths. Slaves also show the ability to plan and execute successful escapes; this alone takes at least a fair amount of intelligence to achieve. Ads also give light to areas of behavior, as previously stated. Some ads tell of drunken behavior while others tell of speech habits or limps.

In the wikipedia article entitled Slavery it says that, “In most institutions of slavery throughout the world, the children of slaves became the property of the master. Local laws varied as to whether the status of the mother or of the father determined the fate of the child; but were usually determined by the status of the mother.” This excerpt shows the cycle of slavery, how a family can lack the opportunity to live a free life or even acquire a free life. Running seems to be the only option left in most cases, an individuals last effort to live a free life.
