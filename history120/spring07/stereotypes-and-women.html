Stereotypes and Women

Stereotypes have been a part of the American culture since Englishmen first evaded the country. Stereotypes can be defined as “ideas held about members of particular groups, based solely on membership in that group” (Wikipedia). We are presented with these stereotypes everyday of our lives, and everyday we are pressured to embed these into our culture and lives. Some of the most common stereotypes have to do with women. Throughout history women have had very specific roles that they were forced to play and that is how society has come to portray women. The “cultural work” that stereotypes did in the nineteenth century is still the same work that is is doing today, which is cause society to view certain groups with these judegements in mind and creates room for even more stereotypes to be made. 

I chose to focus my whole post on the sterotypes that women have and still face today. In my opinion, women have been one of the most discriminated groups in this country throughout  history. The common beliefs behind America was based around men and women struggled to compete. To this day, many women still struggle to maintain their own identities and to create a free life of their own. Consequently, the two stereotypes I focused on are “women are meant to be housewives” and that “being a nurse is a women’s job.”

One of the most significant stereotypes towards women is the fact that “ women are meant to be housewives.” During the nineteenth century, all that women were known for was being housewives and very few actually held positions other than this one. For example, the first picture is an advertisement that does a good job showing this stereotype (Housewife). As the world is fighting the famine, her job, as she is a woman, has to do with providing food. In the second picture (Housewife 2), we see a more modern day housewife with her duster in hand. The biggest difference between the two pictures is that the today’s housewife looks more worried about her make up then anything else. Which in turn yields the fact that today women not only are supposed to be housewives, but also are supposed to always look good and be ready for anything. As well, both women are seen wearing an apron, leading to that being a sign of cleaning. Both of these made life for women hard, whether in the nineteenth century, or today. It made it hard for women to be taken seriously, which makes it hard for them to break out of their shells and become workingwomen. Men view women as these objects at their disposal and view themselves as the powerhouse. All this in turn makes it hard for women to get jobs and be taken seriously. Though the biggest change in this stereotype is that as women then were silent and took their place, women now work hard to make something of themselves.

Women have always been seen as the nurse. In the nineteenth century, most doctors were men and most women were nurses. In the picture (Nurse), we see that the woman is very happy to be a nurse and is the typical portrayal of a nurse. In the more modern drawing (Nurse 2), the little girl is seen playing with her stuffed animal, nurse. This is showing that the stereotype of women being nurturing develops, as women are young children. They are trained to know their role and know that they are meant to be nurses.   In a way, this is stereotypical against men as well. It is saying that men who are nurses are gay and laughed at. For example, in the movie “Meet the parents” the main character is a nurse and is teased for it. As well, this stereotype shows that women are not meant to be doctors and shouldn’t be. The biggest reason that we connect women and nurses is because of the attributes we attribute to women. Women are known to be sweet, tender and caring. As well, they are lovable, sincere and sensitive. All these make women and nurses one. Coincedently that is why we choose to not attribute nurses as being male. Men who are nurses in turn are seen as sensitive and caring, making them look gay and foolish in guyish atmospheres.

            Throughout history women have been stereotyped against. It has been common knowledge that women are meant to be housewives and that only women were meant to be nurses. As a result, many more stereotypes can be formed just from these two alone. Not only can more stereotypes be formed, but also as a culture, we choose to view this group in this sense and think that they cannot be anything else.
