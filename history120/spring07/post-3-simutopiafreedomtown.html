Post #3 Simutopia:Freedomtown

February 27th, 2007 by Neil Kelly

My utopia Freedomtown will enjoy the right to practice whatever religious beliefs they want, so long as their religion abides by our peaceful laws, which strictly prohibit violence, stealing, and any other wrongful acts committed against our people. Citizens who are agnostic or atheist are also welcome in our community too. Citizens who are not respectful of other people’s various religious beliefs will be reprimanded and asked to leave our community if the problem persists.

Our community is set up in a way that encourages individuality and respects the differences of everyone. These differences in ideas and beliefs should be respected by all, and everyone should learn to get along with everyone. Everyone in our town will be regarded as equal, regardless of race, religion, and gender. We would also like to be known as the community with the lowest crime rate. The ultimate goal of Freedomtown is too create a community with a variety of different people with various religious philosophies to all live peacefully together.

Due to our community’s vast differences in beliefs our community realizes the challenges of our town being considered a utopia. However, once people understand the way of life of Freedomtown they will see that it is achievable. Our community encourages the idea of expressing yourself and ideas openly. No religion is seen as right or wrong in the eyes of the community. All townspeople will learn to respect the differences of religious beliefs and opinions, and if certain people cannot abide by this than they will be forced to leave our community.

Freedomtown will be a mix of urban and rural communities. Our town has its industrial factories located across the river separated from our farms, houses, and public buildings. We have a couple of parks and one big one that will be the social gathering spot for our various events. Our community will be a capitalist economy, which will allow people to own businesses and create revenues as long as it is done peacefully without exploiting people. Our businesses will be allowed to import and export goods to and from other communities. Freedomtown will be a mix of agricultural and industrial businesses, and taxes will be administered in order to pay our public officials and to improve our community as necessary. Social events will be held in the parks every weekend, which will allow the town to get together and socialize around music, beer, and dancing. Everyone is expected to go, but the choice belongs to the residents of our community.

Our community will have no one leader in charge instead it will be a leader-by-committee, which will consist of 12 people. The 12 people will be voted in by the people and remain on the board for no longer than 7 years. The rules of Freedomtown are strictly enforced by our town police department. Our town will also have judges that will listen to cases involving residents who have broken laws. The judges will have absolute power and have the ultimate deciding factor of whether or not it is necessary to ban the resident for his act. If a person has been declared banned from the community, he or she has the chance to appeal to a panel of 3 judges. If two out of the three Judges rule in favor of banning the resident than that person must leave Freedomtown within 3 days. A person who is banned may be allowed to come back to the community after 5 years but must have a majority vote approved by the 12 leaders of the community.
