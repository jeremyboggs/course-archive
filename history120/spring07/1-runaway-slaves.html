#1 Runaway Slaves

I read three different slave advertisments. They all had one shared theme running through them. All three advertisements pertained to women who were pregnant. The advertisements suggested that preganant slaves were not treated like women with children, but instead as a piece of property with slightly more value.
The first thing I noticed in the adverstisement was that all of the women were dressed in “old” clothes, and in Virginia made clothes. The old clothes would suggest that their owners did not care enough about them to buy them new clothes. In addition, Virginia-made clothes would suggest that their clothing was made of cotton. Back in the days of slavery, clothes made of cotton were the cheapest clothes one could find. This is both because cotton was very abundant, and also because it is very likely that the slaves made their own clothes out of the cotton they picked.
Another thing which suggested that these women were treated as pure property was the fact that some of them did not have names. In the one advertisement where the woman actually did have a name, the article said “she calls herself PAT.” Her owner in the article continually just referred to her as the “Nego Woman.” This would show that the owner did not care or respect that this woman had an identity; he could not even show her the respect of calling her by her name. This seems esepcially dehumanizing because even most housepets have a name.
In addition, one side thing I noticed was that most of the women were described as being fat. This description was aside from the fact that they were pregant. This could suggest that they were indoor slaves, possibly cooks, or servants inside. The slaves who worked in the cotton fields all day probably tended to be thinner because they were doing hard labor. Also, one of the advertisements had mentioned that the slave woman was wearing a cooks apron, so that would be more evidence to confirm the hypothesis.
Another thing i noticed in the advertisementswas that one of the rewards for finding these slaves was a pistols. This shows that the owners of these slaves owned guns. They could have complete domination over the slaves because they were literally armed. It would take a lot of courage to disobey or leave someone who had the power to shoot you with a gun. Hence at the same time, this fact also shows the strength of spirit that these runway women must have had to leave their owners. They knew the possible consequences of getting caught but they continued on.
The article in Wikipedia said that slave were only seen as property not as humans [1]. Also the article stated that in the south slaves became the backbone of the work force [1]. The ads help enhance my understading of colonial America with regards to the reading for this week because the ads show how many of the slaves owners didnt even call their slaves by their names. This helps prove that slaves were not seen as human. In addition, the fact that owner took the time to write the ads and were willing to pay a very high reward for the slaves return it shows that slaves were in fact very important to the slaves owners.

Therefore, in light of the information contained in both the advertisments and the wikipedia articles, it seems clear that slaves were dehumanized by their owners, and considered soley as property. Their indentities, feelings, and health were not important. It also seems clear that women slaves were not treated any better than males, and pregant female slaves were not treated better than non pregnant female slave either. This was obviously a low point in American history, and I am glad our country has overcome this travesty.
