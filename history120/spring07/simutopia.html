Post #3 Simutopia:Freedomtown »

simutopia

In my utopia, I plan to provide everything for the people living there, with the option to expand; there will never be involuntary unemployment, the crime rate will be minimal, and the residents will generally be in the same socioeconomic class.

The houses in Amical are all in one general area, so that the residents will be able to get to know each other and be able to help each other out easily. The houses are, however, spaced out just enough so that the families will be able get their privacy and not have to worry about the neighbors looking through their windows and stuff like that. The same holds true with the factories and public buildings; they are all grouped together with others like it. Where people live, work, and hang out are all in separate areas. While at work, the workers won’t be able to look out the window and see the shops and start day dreaming. In order for factory made goods to be readily available all the time, the productivity rate must be excellent. So that is why the factories are pretty far away from the shops. But if factory work isn’t for you, then there will always be jobs available at the shops. The park is also nearby the shops so that you can get some quiet time in while running errands or if you just need to get away from everyday life.

Everyone is welcome, and we don’t discriminate. Every house comes with the means to support the family living there: fields, orchards, woods, and easy access to the river. I plan to end unemployment in the community by offering work to both men and women at the factory to the people working there. If the factories were to become crowded, there is plenty of nearby land to build another one. Any disturbances or breaking of the laws will be solved by the police department, which works with the judicial system. Depending on who moves into the town, religion may or may not be a major component of our society. The majority of the residents will also choose which religion to practice at the church. We will have a governor, who is elected by the people, who will be in charge of the town. The governor would also have a board of supervisors under him who will keep him in check while making decisions and passing laws. We want a set up like that so our governor does not receive too much power, or aspire to gain total control. The wealth will be distributed pretty evenly, since everyone has everything they need to live, and an equal chance of work at the factory. Although the life style is not lavish, people will live contently and will never have to go without everyday necessities and comforts. For instance, our people will never miss a meal because they either don’t have the means to make one or cannot afford one. Drinking will also be strictly prohibited, which will be strongly enforced by the police department. Hopefully, this will help increase morale and production rate, while lowering the crime rate.

My goal for this community will be to establish an effective town in which people will have the freedom to provide for themselves, or choose to pay for the things that they need. Alcoholism will not be a problem, so there would be no reason for alcohol to provide unproductive behavior. Women’s rights will be honored with their equal opportunity to employment, if they choose to work. I plan for Amical to be the ideal place to live, where everyone could live contently.
