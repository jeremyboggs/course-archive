Mulatto Slaves exceptionally valuable and better chances of escape.

Mulatto slaves had very useful skills and were considered exceptionally valuable. The chances of escape as a Mulatto Slave where enhanced due to his or her color. As a Mulatto slave, chances of escape where increased since the skin color resembled that of Native Americans. As explained in many advertisements resembling Indians as appose to African American slaves made chances of escape much greater. As many Mulatto slaves disguised themselves as Indians wearing match coats and other Indian apparel, increasing their chances of escape and hiding out amongst Black Indians. Many where found hiding in forests and other wooded areas as they attempted to pass themselves off as Black Indians. It appears that the runaways had to be witty and cunning to portray themselves as freed slaves or a Black Indians.

These ads tell us a great deal about the courage and resilience these men and women had during these times of hardship. Some advertisements had warnings explaining how difficult it was to catch certain slaves. Many would escape again from the slave trackers or would not allow themselves to surrender or be taken alive. These advertisements help show us the mentality and courage of the runways. It took a strong willed person to run-a-way from is his or her master, as these slaves did not know what was waiting for them on the road to freedom. Many Mulatto slaves had acquired skills which were vital to plantations such as carpentry, farming, taming horses, and medicine. Some owners where willing to pay up to two hundred dollars for the return of their runaway slave. At that time that amount of money would equal big bucks today. It is clear that in having such skills as carpentry or horse taming a lot of these slaves could be earning good money as freed slaves.

In most advertisements it seems as if the slave owners are showcasing the talents of the runaway slaves. The way certain texts are written makes it appear as if the slaves made a bad choice and just needed to find there way back to the plantation. They disguise the fact that a human is owned by another human. The Mulatto slaves had a better chance of escape due to their mixed color and their ability to take advantage of that. This history lesson shows us that when people are treated unjustly, they will find ways to rise above it.
