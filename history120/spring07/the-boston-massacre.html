The Boston Massacre

While looking at the module depicting an image of the Boston Massacre, it is clear to see the bias shown against the British, and that the physical state of the British and Americans were very different. The module paints a clear picture for us to visualize the Boston Massacre itself, with such details as where people standing and what it is they are doing. You can see that Captain Preston, the British commander, is standing behind all of his men cheering them on, but not actually fighting himself, or standing in the line of danger. His troops are in a line infront of him all firing their guns at the Americans. The crowd itself is not nearly as organized as the British troops are, in both appearance and actions. They appear to be unarmed, nor are they fighting back, but tending to ones already shot by the British troops. It seems to be early evening, as you can see a small cresent moon in the upper left-hand corner.

One problem with the history of certain events, is that they most often are told from a specific view point, and are therefore biased in some way, shape, or form. By looking at this particular module, it seems Revere wants his viewers to interpret the Boston Massacre as the British making a surprise attack on unarmed Americans, and blatantly making the British out to be the bad guys. With that being said, Revere has also made the American’s appear disorganized by depicting them as a giant mob of people, instead of the organized line the British are standing in. To take it one step further, one could say that this particular group of American’s in Boston are not very patriotic because they do not appear to be actively fighting for their independence from Britain. Because Revere has created this picture, he obviously had a specific story he wanted to tell. Depending on wether it was a British or an American, others may tell the story a completely different way.

Although this module does tell a particular story from a particular point of view, that doesn’t mean that it is not credible. It is simply a portrayal of a part of history, which could very well be accurate. With so many different things going on in the picture, there are many ways to interpret what Revere is trying to depict, some being more obvious than others.
