#7: Women and Cars - Past: Oldsmobile | Future: Formula One Racing

     In the 1950s it was thought that women only care about the elegance, comfort and other visually appealing features of a car. It was never thought that they would take interest in the mechanical and performance aspect of the car. In modern times women are changing the standard for what type of cars they prefer and it has developed into something very different from the 1950s.  Over the decades women have developed a different thinking and outlook on the world of automobiles.
   

     I chose to view the video clip “Oldsmobile What Women Want”. This video seemed to be a training video for 1950s Oldsmobile sales associates. It covered what the associates should focus on when selling cars to women. It portrayed woman as people who only cared about the visually appealing features of a car. The video addressed how women only liked the rich quality and the feel of richness in a car. The wide range of colors was something else that the video said appealed to women when purchasing a car. Also mentioned was that women would be interested in the feeling of the interior fabric design like she would on a dress. From this one can assume that the main attire for women was dresses back then. It also talked about how the seat belts of the car were perfect for women because they would not hook the dress under the handle and tear it. This statement again implying that women’s main clothing was a dress.  The video also stated that if the woman is with her husband then the sales associate would not be able to get all of her attention versus if she was shopping alone. So it seems back then if a couple went shopping for a car then it would be the man who made most of the decisions and the women would just accompany the husband but have no real opinion in what car to buy.
     The other video that I viewed was “Woman Driver: The Katherine Legge Story Promo”. This told the story of Katherine Legge, a British auto racing driver who is the first women to race a Formula One car in the Champ Car World Series. Everyone predicted that she would fail and not prosper and they said she’s a female in a man’s world. But she worked very hard, was dedicated and proved that she could race a Formula Once car. In a game that crushes the strongest men she won the series opener at Long Beach, in doing this she became the first woman to win a major race in North America. She later went on to win her second and third races at Edmonton and San Jose. This story proves that women have come a long way in the automobile world. It shows that they don’t care merely about the looks of the car nowadays, but that they are interested in understanding the mechanics and performance of the car. I also believe the Legge is a good role model for young girls who are interested in race cars and the mechanics of cars in general. Looking at this story it’s obvious that women’s thought on cars has no doubt changed from the 1950s.
     

     The Oldsmobile video really just made women look like dummies when it came to cars. It showed them as someone who had no brains and that the sales associate could sell them cars by fooling them into buying a car because of how nice it looked and how elegant and luxurious it was regardless of the performance side of the car. In the 1950s it was thought that the husband would do all the thinking and talking about cars because females were not supposed to know anything about the topic. But looking at the Katherine Legge video it is apparent and evident that women have a new viewpoint in regard to cars. The Legge story shows how a women doesn’t just want to drive a car because of looks but that some women are really into the mechanics and performance aspect of cars.  In 50 years there has never been an intensely competitive female driver in the race world but with Legge’s appearance that has changed. In the 1950s it would have been unimaginable for a woman to drive a race car especially after looking at the Oldsmobile video.  But Legge has proven that women are also interested in car’s performance and not just the visually appealing and luxurious aspect of cars as they were in the 1950s.
         
     I’m sure though some women still fit into the category of the Oldsmobile video but that’s not the case with many women. Many women nowadays are concerned about the type of performance that their car will give them and that is something they consider when buying a new car. Personally I have a female relative who is very interested in rally cars and she drives a Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution IX.  The Evolution IX is a rally car that is all about performance with very minimal luxury features. So from this we can see that in today’s trends more and more women are favoring the performance and mechanical side of cars versus the elegance and luxury. It is very true that over the decades women have begun to change their perception of cars.
