Was WWII good for our Economy?? HELP!!

Through out high school I have been taught that the second world war was great for our economy. Then I get to college and my “Adam Smith view, Liberal,” says that the war was not good for our economy, here are the reasons why, someone please help me understand, now I’m torn between my thoughts of the war on the economy. In high school I was taught that the war was good because there was a decline in the unemployment rates. However, my professor told us that the times during the war were not standard times, and that 18% of the jobs created were by the drafting of military members. Also he said that you cannot count women working during the war because they would not have chose to do so. Over all he said that this claim is false because the employment created was not promoting well being of the people so it doesn’t count.

In high school I was also taught that there was a great increase in the measure of real gnp, but he disarms this idea with saying that the production of war materials such as tanks and guns do should not count in the GNP, only ‘butter,’ the things that would be produced in normal peace times. So here are some things that my professor has tried to discredit, Im lost for what the right answer is, maybe its a matter of perception.
